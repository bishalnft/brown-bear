About Brown Bear

The brown bear (Ursus arctos) is a large bear species found across Eurasia and North America. In North America, the populations of brown bears are called grizzly bears, while the subspecies that inhabits the Kodiak Islands of Alaska is known as the Kodiak bear.

Habitat:

Brown bears can be found in many habitats, from the fringes of deserts to high mountain forests and ice fields.

Diet:

Brown bears are omnivorous, eating a mixed diet of plants, berries, fish, and small mammals.

Brown bears aren't just brown. Some are cream or black.

Why are Brown Bear's Important?

Given their dependence on large natural areas, brown bears are important management indicators for a number of other wildlife species. Brown bears also play important roles as predators who keep other animal populations in check. Additionally, they act as seed dispersers, helping to sustain their own environment.

Why are Brown Bear's Endangered (not up to the mark)?

Formerly hunted for its hides, meat, and as a big game trophy, the brown bear's most severe threats are currently habitat destruction and persecution.
